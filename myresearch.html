Research footsteps

Throughout my affair with Science, I have involved myself in seemingly unconnected topics. Although they seem to be distant, over the years I have found out that the learnings from one field can add valuable insight into the other. With this thought in mind, I seek to explore new ideas in the field of Mechanical engineering, Aerospace and Biology, and hope that these dots connect in shaping my career towards Astrobiology.

I finished my bachelors in Mechanical Engineering from Assam Engineering College, India in 2015, and then moved to New York to study Biomechanics at the State University of New York, Stony Brook. This is where I realised that a biology laboratory is more suitable for me than a mechanical workshop. After spending 2.5 years in New York, I decided to move to Switzerland and took a PhD position in ETH Zurich. Here, I transitioned into the field of Plant Biology while at the same time, implementing the elements of engineering in my research and other side projects. Phd Topic

I study the kinematics of mechanotransduction in the sensory hairs of the Venus flytrap, and investigate how the mechanical stimulus imparted by a moving insect is transduced into electrical signals. To do this, I combine biomechanical experiments, imaging and numerical simulations, to develop multi-scale models of the sensory hair. With this, I uncover the cellular mechanics governing mechanotransduction.

Published Papers

1. A single touch can provide sufficient mechanical stimulation to trigger Venus flytrap closure. [July 10, 2020]
2. Kinematics Governing Mechanotransduction in the Sensory Hair of the Venus flytrap. [December 30, 2020]
